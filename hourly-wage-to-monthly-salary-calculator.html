What Is an Hourly Wage to Monthly Salary Calculator?

An hourly wage to monthly salary calculator converts an hourly rate into an estimated monthly salary. It helps you understand how much an hourly job may be worth over a full month or year.

This is useful because hourly pay can be harder to compare with salary-based jobs. A job that pays $18 per hour, $22 per hour, or $30 per hour may sound simple, but the real monthly income depends on weekly hours, paid weeks, overtime, and deductions.

How to Use This Hourly Wage to Monthly Salary Calculator

  • Choose your currency: dollar, euro, pound, or forint.
  • Enter hourly wage: this is your pay rate before taxes.
  • Enter regular weekly hours: use the number of hours you normally work each week.
  • Enter paid weeks per year: use 52 for a full year of paid work.
  • Add overtime if needed: enter overtime hours and the overtime multiplier.
  • Add optional deductions: this can help estimate a rough monthly amount after deductions.
  • Compare pay frequencies: see weekly, biweekly, semimonthly, and monthly amounts.

Why Monthly Salary Matters

Monthly income is one of the most useful numbers for everyday budgeting. Many major expenses are monthly, including rent, mortgage payments, utilities, subscriptions, insurance, loan payments, and savings goals.

If you only look at your hourly wage, it can be difficult to know how much room you have in your budget. Converting hourly pay to monthly salary makes it easier to compare income with monthly expenses.

Gross Pay vs Take-Home Pay

Gross pay is the amount you earn before deductions. Take-home pay, sometimes called net pay, is what remains after taxes and deductions are removed.

Common deductions may include income tax, payroll tax, health insurance, retirement contributions, union dues, benefit costs, wage garnishments, or other employer-specific deductions. Because these vary widely, this calculator focuses on gross pay and lets you enter an optional monthly deduction estimate.

Using This Calculator for Job Offers

When comparing job offers, the hourly rate is only one part of the decision. Two jobs with the same hourly wage can produce different monthly income if the hours, overtime rules, paid time off, benefits, commute costs, or schedule stability are different.

  • Compare the estimated yearly salary, not only the hourly rate.
  • Check whether the job offers consistent hours each week.
  • Consider unpaid time off, seasonal work, and reduced hours.
  • Include commute costs when comparing two job locations.
  • Look at benefits, health insurance, retirement matching, and paid leave.

Common Mistakes When Converting Hourly Wage to Monthly Salary

  • Using four weeks per month: a year has about 4.33 weeks per month, not exactly 4.
  • Ignoring unpaid time off: unpaid weeks reduce annual and monthly income.
  • Counting overtime as guaranteed: overtime may change from week to week.
  • Confusing gross and net pay: taxes and deductions can reduce take-home income.
  • Ignoring variable schedules: part-time or shift-based work may not provide the same hours every week.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate monthly salary from hourly wage?

Multiply hourly wage by weekly hours, then multiply by paid weeks per year. Divide the yearly amount by 12 to estimate monthly gross salary.

Is this calculator before or after taxes?

The main result is gross pay before taxes and deductions. You can enter optional monthly deductions to create a rough adjusted monthly estimate, but this is not a full tax calculation.

Why is monthly pay not just weekly pay times four?

A year has 52 weeks. When divided by 12 months, the average month has about 4.33 weeks. Using only four weeks can underestimate monthly income.

Can I use this calculator for part-time work?

Yes. Enter your part-time hours per week and your hourly wage. The calculator will estimate weekly, monthly, and yearly gross pay.

Can I include overtime?

Yes. Enter overtime hours per week and the overtime multiplier. For example, use 1.5 for time-and-a-half.
