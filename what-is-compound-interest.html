What Is Compound Interest? (In Simple Terms)

Compound interest means you earn interest on your interest. Your balance grows faster because both your original deposit and the interest you‚Äôve already earned keep generating new interest.

Key Factors That Influence Your Growth

1. Time

The longer your money is invested, the more dramatic the compounding effect becomes.

2. Interest rate

Even a small increase in rate (for example, 6% to 8%) can make a huge difference over decades.

3. Compounding frequency

Daily compounding grows slightly faster than monthly, which grows faster than annual compounding. But over very long periods, time and consistency matter more than frequency.

4. Regular contributions

Even $50‚Äì$100 per month can turn into tens of thousands of dollars if you let it grow for long enough.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Waiting too long to start investing or saving
  • Stopping contributions when markets drop
  • Keeping all savings in very low-interest accounts long-term
  • Trying to perfectly time the market instead of staying invested
