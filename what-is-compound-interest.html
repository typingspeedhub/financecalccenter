What Is Compound Interest?

Compound interest means your money earns interest, and that interest itself earns interest. Over time, this creates exponential growth rather than simple, linear growth.

It applies to savings accounts, investments, retirement accounts, and even some debts.

The Compound Interest Formula

The standard formula looks like this:

FV = P √ó (1 + r / n)(n √ó t)

  • P = initial principal
  • r = annual interest rate
  • n = compounding frequency
  • t = time in years

Frequently Asked Questions

Is compound interest guaranteed?

No. Returns depend on the account type, interest rate, and market conditions.

How often should interest compound?

More frequent compounding leads to slightly higher returns, but time matters far more.

Does compound interest apply to debt?

Yes ‚Äî credit cards and some loans compound interest against you.

Is APY the same as compound interest?

APY reflects compounding, while APR usually does not.
