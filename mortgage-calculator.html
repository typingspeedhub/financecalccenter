How this mortgage calculator works

The principal and interest part of your payment is calculated using a standard amortizing loan formula:

  • Principal: home price minus down payment
  • Interest rate: fixed annual rate for the full term
  • Term: number of years of the mortgage

Property taxes and homeowner‚Äôs insurance are converted to monthly amounts and added on top of the principal & interest payment, along with any HOA fees you enter.

What Is a Mortgage Calculator?

How Mortgage Payments Work (Simple Explanation)

Most mortgages are amortizing loans. That means your payment is designed so the loan balance reaches zero by the end of the term. Early in the loan, a larger share of each payment typically goes to interest. Over time, more of your payment goes toward the principal balance.

Even small changes to the interest rate or term can significantly change the total interest you pay, which is why comparing scenarios can be useful before you commit.

Tips for Using This Mortgage Calculator

  • Compare interest rates: even a 0.25% difference can add up over decades.
  • Test different down payments: a larger down payment reduces the loan amount (and often the interest).
  • Include realistic taxes & insurance: these can materially affect affordability.
  • Watch total cost: a lower monthly payment (longer term) can mean higher total interest.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this mortgage calculator accurate?

It provides an estimate based on the values you enter and a standard fixed-rate mortgage formula. Your actual payment may differ due to lender fees, escrow policies, rounding, and local tax/insurance costs.

Does this include PMI (mortgage insurance)?

No. This estimate does not include PMI. If your down payment is below a lender‚Äôs threshold (often 20%), you may need to add PMI to your monthly cost.

Does this include closing costs?

No. Closing costs are typically paid upfront (or rolled into the loan in some cases) and are not included in the monthly estimate.

What is the difference between ‚Äúmonthly payment (P&I)‚Äù and ‚Äútotal monthly payment‚Äù?

P&I is just principal plus interest. Total monthly payment adds estimated property taxes, homeowner‚Äôs insurance, and any HOA fees you enter.
