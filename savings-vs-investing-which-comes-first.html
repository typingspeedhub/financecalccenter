Why Saving Comes First (For Most People)

Investing without savings is risky. If an emergency happens, you may be forced to sell investments at the worst possible time.

Before investing, you should have:

  • At least $1,000 in emergency cash
  • No high-interest credit card debt
  • Predictable monthly expenses covered
Example:
You invest $5,000 in the market. A $1,500 car repair happens. If the market is down, you lock in losses just to pay the bill.

Savings buy you time and stability. Investing without that safety net creates stress and poor decisions.

When Does Investing Come First?

Savings should not be exposed to market risk. Investments should not be used for short-term needs.
