How Compound Interest Works (Simple Explanation)

With simple interest, you earn interest only on your initial amount. With compound interest, each period‚Äôs interest is added to your balance, so future interest is calculated on a larger total. Over time, this can create a snowball effect‚Äîespecially when you keep your money invested and add contributions regularly.

In general, longer time horizons and consistent contributions often have a bigger impact than small differences in short-term returns.

Why Compound Interest Is So Powerful

Compound interest rewards two things more than anything else: time and consistency. Starting earlier gives your money more time to grow, and steady contributions can significantly increase the amount that compounds. That‚Äôs why many long-term plans focus on regular saving and investing rather than trying to chase perfect timing.
