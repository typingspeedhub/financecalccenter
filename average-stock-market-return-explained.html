Average Stock Market Return Explained

A clear explanation of historical returns, inflation impact, volatility, and realistic long-term investing assumptions.

What Is the Average Stock Market Return?

Historically, the U.S. stock market has returned roughly 7%–10% per year on average over long periods. This includes price growth and dividends.

Nominal vs Real Returns

Nominal return is the raw percentage gain. Real return adjusts for inflation.

If inflation averages 3% and your investments return 10%, your real return is roughly 7%.

Why Returns Vary Year to Year

Markets are volatile. Some years deliver +20%, others -15%. The long-term average smooths out this volatility.
