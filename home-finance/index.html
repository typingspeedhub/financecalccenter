Why home finance planning matters

Housing is one of the largest expenses in most household budgets. A mortgage payment includes more than the amount borrowed, and even a small change in the interest rate can affect the monthly payment and the total amount repaid over many years.

Understanding affordability, interest costs, loan terms, and the difference between renting and buying can help you compare options more carefully before making a long-term financial commitment.

How to use these mortgage tools and guides

Start with the topic that matches your current decision. Use the Mortgage Calculator to estimate payments, then review the guides to understand how interest rates, loan terms, and affordability can change the result.

Calculator results are estimates rather than offers from a lender. Actual mortgage costs may also include taxes, insurance, fees, maintenance, and other housing expenses that should be considered separately.

What you can explore in this section

This Home Finance section brings together practical tools and beginner-friendly explanations about mortgage payments, fixed and variable interest rates, housing affordability, and the financial differences between renting and owning a home.

The goal is to help you understand the numbers behind common housing decisions so that you can compare possible costs using realistic assumptions and verify important figures before taking action.
